## Journalism

![AP Stylebook](/pictures/apStylebook.JPG)

To be truthful, I used to hate writing with a passion. Becoming an author of any sort, whether of a book or for a newspaper, had always been out of the question. In my mind, writing was but a tedious, academic exercise, the study of which was useless besides to earn a good grade in English class.

Having said that, I can't remember why exactly I chose to join Nordic News, our school newspaper. Whatever the reason, I am so happy I did, because Nordic allowed me to finally appreciate the art of writing and communication. Even though I've only been in it for a year thus far, it already has a special place in my heart for how drastically it expanded my horizons.

Many read the news on a daily basis, but few ever think about *writing* the news. It sounds daunting, but to me, newswriting does not require that one obtain a degree from journalism school and land a job at the Associated Press; at its heart, journalism is simply about gathering information and telling a story. It comes down to how one compiles and presents the research they've conducted. This is key -- if you ask 100 journalists to write an article about the same exact topic, you will end up with 100 different articles. There are infinitely many different ways to approach any given subject, and that is precisely why I love journalism.

[Weiju Wang - Nordic News](https://nordicnews.net/staff_name/weiju-wang/)
