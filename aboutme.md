### Journalism

To be truthful, I used to hate writing with a passion. Becoming an author of any sort, whether of a book or for a newspaper, had always been out of the question; in my mind, writing was but a tedious, academic exercise, the study of which was useless besides to earn a good grade in English class.

I can't remember why exactly I chose to join Nordic News, our school newspaper -- and it wasn't because I liked journalism or anything close to it -- but whatever the reason, I am so happy I did. Nordic allowed me to appreciate the art of writing and communication, and even though I've only been in it for a year thus far, it has a special place in my heart for how much it broadened my skills and interests.

Many read the news on a daily basis, but few ever think about *writing* the news, as a journalist does. This sounds daunting, but to me, being a journalist does not require that one obtain a degree from journalism school and land a job at the Associated Press; even writing for a small blog, like I do in [my news blog](newsblog.md), is sufficient. At its heart, journalism is about gathering information and telling a story. It comes down to how one presents the research they have conducted, and how they put two and two together. This is what I love about journalism; if you ask 100 journalists to write articles about the same exact topic, you will end up with 100 different articles. There are infinitely many different ways to approach any given subject, and that is what makes journalism -- and writing as a whole -- interesting.
